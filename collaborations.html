Women in STEM Workshop
With Team iGEM Concordia, Team iGEM Thessaloniki and Team iGEM Patras


Figure 1: Description ...
iGEM Concordia is the team we have collaborated with most and for the third year in a row collaborating with them, we decided to create a workshop about women in STEM and how to make research and iGEM teams more diverse and inclusive. To include more teams in this event, we also decided to create the virtual bulletin board mentioned above.

When we joined forces with team Patras and Thessaloniki, we agreed to collaborate on the workshop as well. This collaboration also extended to an AfteriGEM organisation, WiSTEM, who helped us with the introduction of our event. Eve Langelier, head of Canada’s chair of women in science and engineering, gave us essential feedback on our workshop as well as giving us ressources we could share to the attendees.

Our workshop was on september 16th and around 25 people attended. Attendees were given the ressources Eve Langelier gave us and they gave us very positive feedback on our workshop.
Rosalind Chronicles
With Team iGEM Concordia, Team iGEM Thessaloniki and Team iGEM Patras


This project first started as a collaboration exclusively with Team iGEM Concordia and took the form of a bulletin board describing inspiring women in STEM from across the world. It was planned as the first part of a two part collaboration with Team Concordia, the second part being the workshop described below.

Figure 1: Description ...
After promoting this first part collaboration, a member of our team noticed that, two days later, two other iGEM teams were organising a similar collaboration, teams Patras and Thessaloniki. Seeing the perfect opportunity for a larger collaboration, we contacted those teams and all four teams engaged to create this collaborative document, and the presentation mentioned below.

Our collaboration, called the Rosalind Chronicles, invited other iGEM teams to submit a short description of a woman in STEM from their country and a photo of the women in their team. In total, 26 teams submitted a biography, which allowed us to create an interactive online bulletin board.
JulyGEM
By Team iGEM Calgary
JulyGEM is a yearly event organised by Team iGEM Calgary which allows teams to tune in to workshops about science communication, entrepreneurship and human practices. They also allow teams to participate in pitch sessions, quick, 5 minute presentations about their project followed by questions from judges and feedback from other iGEM teams and the judges themselves.

Given that this presentation was essential for our project last year, we decided to participate again. Even if our project is the same as last year’s, we were still able to give more information and context to attendees on our project and the questions from the judges were very useful, especially for the legal considerations of our project. This was the perfect opportunity to meet new iGEM teams and to learn more about what their projects are about this year.
iGEM Monument Challenge
By Team iGEM Calgary
This initiative, started by Team iGEM Paris-Saclay, challenges iGEM teams to take picture of monuments in the city, post the images in instagram with information on the monuments and challenge other teams to participate. Our team was challenged by Team iGEM Sorbonne and three of our team members went to Quebec City to show four famous monuments in the only fortified city in Canada and the USA.
